Stock Market Basics

The stock market is a marketplace where investors buy and sell shares of publicly traded companies. Stocks represent ownership in a company, and their value fluctuates based on company performance, economic conditions, and investor sentiment.

Investors make money in two ways: **capital appreciation** (when stock prices increase) and **dividends** (profits shared with shareholders).

Key concepts in stock market investing include:

  • Blue-chip stocks: Large, well-established companies known for stability (e.g., Apple, Microsoft).
  • Growth stocks: Companies expected to grow at an above-average rate (e.g., Tesla, Amazon).
  • Value stocks: Undervalued companies with strong fundamentals.
  • Index funds & ETFs: Low-cost investment options that track market indexes (e.g., S&P 500).

Understanding **risk management** and **diversification** is crucial for successful investing.

Personal Budgeting

Personal budgeting is the process of planning income and expenses to achieve financial goals. A well-structured budget helps avoid debt, save for future needs, and manage daily expenses effectively.

Common budgeting methods:

  • 50/30/20 Rule: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings/debt payments.
  • Zero-based budgeting: Every dollar is assigned a purpose, ensuring nothing is wasted.
  • Envelope system: Physical cash is allocated to different spending categories.

Tracking expenses and adjusting the budget as income or costs change is essential for long-term financial health.

Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency that uses blockchain technology for secure and decentralized transactions. Unlike traditional money, cryptocurrencies operate without a central authority like a bank.

Popular cryptocurrencies:

  • Bitcoin (BTC): The first and most well-known cryptocurrency, often referred to as "digital gold."
  • Ethereum (ETH): Known for its smart contract functionality, enabling decentralized applications.
  • Stablecoins: Cryptos like USDT or USDC, designed to maintain a stable value tied to fiat currencies.

Investing in crypto requires understanding **market volatility**, **wallet security**, and **regulatory risks**.
